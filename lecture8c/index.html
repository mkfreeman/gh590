Assessment framework

Goal of user centered design

“Design the right product for the right people”
  • Identify target population for product (users)
  • Construct aggregate representations of common users (personas)
  • Describe contexts in which product is used (scenarios)
  • Detail specific tasks to accomplish (use cases)

Design process (personas and scenarios)

  • Do your personas represent your user base?
  • Have you identified the most important issues to your personas?
  • Are your scenarios realistic?

Implementation (use cases)

  • Can your visualization answer the questions of your personas?
  • How easily can people answer the questions of your personas?

Evaluating use cases (self assessment)

  • Can the visualization present the data of interest?
    • Are the options available to display values
    • How many steps does it take to answer questions of interest?
  • Graphical perception
    • Nominal/ordinal: are differences between categories distinguishable?
    • Quantitative: how well can magnitude be estimated?

Evaluation surveys

  • Derive specific questions from scenarios of personas
  • Assess time, self rated difficulty
  • Graphical perception: how easily can values be estimated?

Why should people love maps?

  • Rapid visual processing
  • Geospatial pattern recognition
  • Outlier detection

Why shouldn't people love maps?

  • Encodings are difficult to process
  • Large countries are the focus
  • Small countries are marginalized
  • Non geospatial patterns are harder to detect

Projections

Projections translate 3D surfaces into cartesian coordinates

  • Enhances usability, portability, interpretability
  • Obscures one or more of these features
    • Shape
    • Area
    • Distance
    • Bearing

Uses of maps

Show single values for each entity

  • Color
  • Distortion
  • Overlapping shape

Show connections between entities

Show overlay of other entities

Sketching

Considering maps or alternative representations

  • 5 datasets
  • Jot down visualization purpose
  • Sketch visual encoding
  • Keep in mind:
    • Specific question you want to answer
    • Transformations of data
    • Issues of scale
    • Multiple (integrated?) charts
    • Leverage interactivity
